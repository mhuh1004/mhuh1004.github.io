Physical Mixtape Final

For the final of the class IDEA215: Introduction to Sensors, Measurement, and Data Analysis taught by professor Daniel Moller at Wesleyan University, we were given free reign to create any type of machine that took some type of numerical data from sensors to create some type of measured output. For the idea planning process, I discovered that my project partner Josh and I both had a love for Japanese city pop music. We both though it would be interesting to create a "physical mix tape" that introduced a new form of giving customized mixtapes as gifts. Taking inspiration from old fashioned jukeboxes and Nintendo Amiibos, we created a music box that would scan "song cards" with customized art representing the song that is on the card. When a certain song card is placed on top of the box, the bar code on its base would be scanned by a camera sensor that would then use a raspberry pi connected to the internet to play the song.
